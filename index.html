2) RSA Cryptography

RSA is an asymmetric cryptography algorithm. It uses two keys: Public key (encrypt) and Private key (decrypt).

Interesting

This section highlights key points about cryptography: history, theory, and applications.

History

  • Classical ciphers were used in wars and secret communication.
  • Caesar Cipher is one of the oldest known substitution ciphers.
  • RSA was published in 1977 and became a base for internet security.

Theory

  • Classical crypto often uses the same key for encrypt and decrypt.
  • RSA uses two keys: public key and private key (asymmetric).
  • RSA security relies on the difficulty of factoring large numbers.

Application

  • HTTPS websites use public-key cryptography for secure connections.
  • Online banking and digital payments use encryption.
  • Digital signatures help verify identity and prevent tampering.
